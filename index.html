About Me

I work on reliability in learning systems: when environments shift, uncertainty spikes, or feedback becomes noisy, models can become overconfident, brittle, or unstable. My interest is in building evaluation and design principles that make learning systems more dependable in real settings.

A recurring theme in my work is bridging trustworthiness (uncertainty + calibration) with autonomy (planning + control), using simulation-driven validation and reliability-aware constraints.
