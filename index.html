About Me

I am a researcher and engineer working at the intersection of Trustworthy AI, Behavioral Meta-Learning, and Reliability-Aware Autonomous Systems. My work integrates applied machine learning, AI reliability evaluation, and safety-centric design for autonomous aerial systems. I am equally committed to community-driven education, having taught and mentored learners across rural, urban, and underserved regions.

I care about AI systems that are not only performant but also reliable, interpretable, and behaviourally stable. My research and outreach emphasize human-centered thinking, continuous learning, and responsible innovation.

Behavioral Reliability in Learning Systems (PGAR)

Independent Research (2024–present)

PGAR is a behavioral meta-learning concept focusing on how learning systems adjust their behavior over time.

  • How models behave under shifting uncertainty
  • How calibration and confidence drift during learning
  • How supervisory behavioral cues support more stable adaptation
